Security policy

The ThreatPulse Radar security policy. Reports of suspected vulnerabilities are welcome and acknowledged within 5 business days.

1. How to report

Send a written report to contact@namanp.de. The same address covers general, privacy and vulnerability-disclosure correspondence; no separate alias is used.

2. Information that helps a report

A high-quality report includes:

  • A clear description of the issue and its impact.
  • A reproducer (curl, browser steps, or a minimal test case).
  • The affected version (commit SHA or release tag).
  • Whether the issue is already known to you.
  • Your contact details and a preferred disclosure timeline.

3. Good-faith expectations

Reports are welcome from anyone acting in good faith on defensive research. The following expectations apply to any testing:

  • No destructive testing. Do not delete, modify or destroy production data, datasets or backups.
  • No privacy violations. Do not attempt to access another visitor's data; the application does not retain any visitor data on the server in the first place.
  • No service disruption. Do not perform load testing or denial-of-service testing against the production deployment. Use a local copy of the application (see docs/v6-3-hostinger.md) for performance work.
  • No social engineering. Do not target operators, contributors or third-party providers.
  • Confidentiality. Do not publicly disclose the issue before the operator has had a reasonable opportunity to investigate and release a fix.

4. Acknowledgement and response

The operator commits to:

  • An acknowledgement within 5 business days of receipt.
  • A status update every 14 days until the issue is resolved or a fix is shipped.
  • Coordinated public disclosure with a credit to the reporter unless the reporter requests anonymity.

5. No bug bounty

The V6.9 milestone does not include a bug-bounty programme. Reports are acknowledged and addressed, but no monetary reward is offered.

6. Out of scope

The following categories are out of scope:

  • Issues that require the visitor to disable the browser's same-origin policy (e.g. by pasting arbitrary JavaScript into the devtools console).
  • Issues that require a malicious browser extension to be installed.
  • Issues that require physical access to a Hostinger data-centre machine.
  • Theoretical attacks that require a hostile network operator to actively MITM a TLS connection (the production CSP, HSTS and SameSite cookies are the documented mitigations).

7. Supported versions

The V6.x line is the supported security line. Earlier lines are end-of-life and are not eligible for new security updates.
