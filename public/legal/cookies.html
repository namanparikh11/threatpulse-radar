Cookie & storage notice

The result of the V6.9 cookie and storage audit, the consent-model decision, and the evidence basis for it.

1. Cookies

No cookies are set, read or required at any point in the V6.9 build.

The audit covered:

  • document.cookie (read) — absent in every src/**, hostinger/**, server/** and jobs/** source file.
  • Set-Cookie (response header) — absent in every hostinger/ and server/ route handler.
  • cookieStore (Cookie Store API) — absent.
  • Cookie request header — never consumed server-side; the browser never sends one because the application never sets one.

3. Third-party browser dependencies

No third-party scripts, stylesheets, fonts, iframes, embeds, analytics, tracking, telemetry, pixels, beacons or fingerprinting libraries are loaded by the V6.9 build.

The only third-party browser-direct fetch() targets that can fire from the production bundle are the documented live-data fallback origins:

  • https://www.cisa.gov — CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog
  • https://services.nvd.nist.gov — NVD CVE 2.0 API
  • https://api.first.org — FIRST EPSS API
  • https://osv.dev — OSV.dev vulnerability database

These four fallbacks fire only when the primary /api/dataset route is unreachable, and they receive no visitor authentication, no identifying cookies and no fingerprint beyond the standard User-Agent and Accept headers. They are an availability fallback for the same public-upstream pipeline that the operator's server-side refresh scheduler already runs every 30 minutes.

4. Consent model decision

No consent banner is implemented and none is required. The audit found zero cookies and zero non-essential storage; every browser storage primitive in use is necessary for a documented product feature.

The application does not call any consent management platform, does not load any third-party consent script and does not track the visitor for the purpose of recording a refusal. The dataSource, mode and publicStateFingerprint fields on the dataset envelope are the application-internal provenance signals used by the dashboard's "Data route" status pill; they are not consent signals and are not user-visible privacy controls.

5. How to clear the data

Every local surface has a documented "Clear" action gated by a typed confirmation. The same surfaces also support "Export" to a verified SHA-256-checksummed JSON backup before clearing. A full reset is available through the browser's native "Clear site data" / "Clear cookies and site data" controls.

6. Evidence

The 29-assertion verification suite scripts/verify-v69-privacy-and-runtime-hardening.mjs is the machine-readable evidence for every claim above. The full audit + decision record is in the privacy notice and in docs/v6-9-privacy-cookie-and-security-hardening.md.
